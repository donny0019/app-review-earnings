Top 5 Types of Apps You Can Get Paid to Test in 2025

When you think of "getting paid to review apps," what comes to mind? While many opportunities exist, the world of app testing is incredibly diverse. The feedback a gaming developer needs is vastly different from what a banking app requires.

Understanding these categories can help you find your niche and provide more valuable feedback, leading to higher earnings. Here are the top 5 types of apps you can get paid to test right now.

1. Mobile Games

This is the largest and most popular category. From hyper-casual puzzlers to complex RPGs, developers are constantly looking for real players to test their games before launch.

What Developers Look For: Bug reports (e.g., "My character got stuck on the wall in Level 3"), gameplay balance, how "fun" the core loop is, and feedback on monetization (e.g., "The price for the gem pack feels too high for the reward").

Keyword Focus: test mobile games for money

2. Productivity & Utility Apps

This category includes everything from to-do lists and note-taking apps to file converters and custom keyboard apps. The focus here is on efficiency and functionality.

What Developers Look For: How easily a user can accomplish their primary task. Is the workflow intuitive? Do the features save time? Is the app faster or more convenient than its competitors?

3. FinTech & Banking Apps

Financial Technology is a booming industry. These apps handle real money, so security, trust, and clarity are paramount.

What Developers Look For: Testers who can spot confusing transaction histories, security loopholes, and unclear fee structures. They want to ensure users feel their money is safe and that the app is trustworthy. This is often a higher-paying review category due to the sensitivity of the app.

4. Health & Wellness Apps

This includes fitness trackers, meditation guides, calorie counters, and mental health platforms.

What Developers Look For: Accuracy of data tracking (e.g., "The step counter seemed to be 10% lower than my phone's built-in tracker"), effectiveness of motivational features, ease of logging data, and the overall positive impact on the user's well-being.

5. Social Media & Creator Apps

These are new platforms trying to compete with giants like TikTok and Instagram, or tools designed to help creators on those platforms.

What Developers Look For: Feedback on content discovery algorithms (what makes the feed engaging?), ease of creating and uploading content, moderation of harmful content, and the appeal of unique features designed to build a community.
